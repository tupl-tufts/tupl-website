What is TuPL

TuPL is the superset of all research into programming languages being done at Tufts University. Historically this includes RedLine Systems research at Tufts as directed by Sam Guyer, the Siriusly research grant on which Kathleen's and Sam's graduate students have worked on research into the foundations of embedded domain specific languages, and undergraduate curricular development in the form of foundational programming languages educational material developed by Norman Ramsey for Tufts' core programming languages course. We also welcomed our newest faculty member, Jeff Foster, to the elements of our TuPL in the Fall of 2018.

TuPL Projects

Autobahn Inferring strictness annotations in Haskell programs using a genetic algorithm.
PADS Domain specific language for processing ad-hoc data implemented in C and Haskell.
antlr-haskell Port of ANTLR to Haskell as a quasiquoted embedded domain specific language.
AllStar Work on a provably correct implementation of the ALL(*) parsing algorithm.
Butter A distributed computation DSL for easily spreading around monadic computation in Haskell.
Elephant Tracks A garbage collection tracing tool for Java programs.
Heapviz Tool for visualizing and exploring snapshots of the heap obtained from running Java programs.
